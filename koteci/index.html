01

A shop the size of a good afternoon

Koteci is one small room of English books, nearly all of them second-hand. Some came from people leaving Tirana, some from readers clearing a shelf, most still carry the marks of whoever owned them first.

There is no system you need to learn. You wander, you pull things off the shelf, you ask what is worth reading. And somewhere in here, usually on the warmest pile, the cat is asleep. He was here before the till and he intends to outlast it.

Open most days, closed when the rain is good for reading.

02

What you find on the shelves

No two visits look the same. The stock turns over as fast as people read, so this is less a catalogue than a rough idea of what is usually waiting.

  • 01

    Used English books

    Paperbacks and hardbacks that have already been read once, priced so you can take a chance on something new.

  • 02

    Rare finds

    Out-of-print titles, odd editions and the occasional book you had given up looking for. They come and go quickly.

  • 03

    Owner picks

    A small shelf of books we have actually read and would hand to a friend, with a line or two on why.

  • 04

    Literary fiction

    The heart of the shop. Novels and short stories, modern and old, in whatever turned up this week.

  • 05

    Cosy browsing

    No rush, no soundtrack of someone selling to you. Stay as long as you like and read the first chapter standing up.

  • 06

    A resident cat

    He does not help you choose, but he will judge you for it. Saying hello is free and, frankly, expected.

03

The cat who runs the place

Every good bookshop has one thing it cannot explain in a listing. Ours has a tail. He picks the visitors he likes, sleeps through the rest, and has never once apologised for sitting on the book you wanted.

People come back for him as much as the books, which is fine by us. He works for warmth and the occasional scratch behind the ear, and he has been doing it longer than any of us.
